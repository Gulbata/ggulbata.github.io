About us

The name and the concept of the restaurant is connected with the famous Georgian artist and one of the founders of Georgian modernism - Petre Otskheli and his famous painting “Flying Painter”. We offer an interpretation of traditional Georgian cuisine and tastes in a totally unique way, seeking to modernize classic flavors while staying true to its roots. An exclusive selection of premium-quality Georgian wines is an absolute highlight of Lendav Maaler. Here you can find carefully selected Qvevri wines by small, family-owned wineries. For the summertime, there’s also a cozy green terrace hidden from the streets, where one can enjoy warm summer evenings. At Lendav Maaler, Georgia is presented with a slightly different angle, and more attention is paid to the modern urban character, both in food and design. Our aim is to introduce you to contemporary Georgian culture with often organized music-related events that vary from Dj’s to live music, as well as wine tastings and Georgian art-related evenings.

Our best Specialties

One of the special types of the famous Khachapuri, so-called “Cheese-boat” is probably not that unfamiliar to you,but what might be unfamiliar is the technique we are using for baking it.Adjarian Khachapuri comes from the Adjara region, which is famous for its totally different type of Khachapuri, which also developed the “no-dough” version not so many years ago. This is the technique we are bringing to you, removing the extra dough from the inside, which leaves the dough crusty and more space for cheese.

Drinks

Georgia is the oldest wine producing region in the world. The fertile valleys and protective slopes of the South Caucasus were home to grapevine cultivation and neolithic wine production (Georgian: ღვინო, vino) for at least 8000 years. Due to millennia of winemaking and the prominent economic role it retains in Georgia to the present day, wine and viticulture are entwined with Georgia's national identity.
