Our special Collection of Georgian Wines

Georgia is the birthplace of wine. The oldest residual evidence of winemaking was found on fragments of 8,000-year-old Qvevri (or Kvevri) in Georgia. It is a uniquely Georgian clay vessel used for wine fermentation, maturation, and storage. In 2013 UNESCO added the Qvevri winemaking tradition to its catalog of humanity’s intangible cultural heritage. The white wines, fermented in Qvevri with their skins for months, resulting in powerful tannins and dark amber color. In Georgia, there are more than 500 endemic grape varieties, most of them are forgotten and you will rarely find them in the vineyards of small wineries and family cellars. The third collection of wine people “Georgian Rare” serves to restore the forgotten wine grape varieties and introduce them to wine lovers and enthusiasts.
